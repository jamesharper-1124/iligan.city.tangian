The Iligan City Jail
The Iligan City Jail is a government correctional facility under the Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP). It houses persons deprived of liberty (PDLs) awaiting trial or serving sentences for minor offenses. The jail offers rehabilitation programs and aims to provide a secure environment for inmates while upholding their human rights.
The Iligan City Jail plays a multifaceted role within the city's justice system. It functions as a secure facility for detaining individuals awaiting trial or serving sentences for minor offenses. But its significance extends beyond mere confinement. By offering rehabilitation programs and upholding human rights, the jail strives to equip inmates with the tools and support they need to reintegrate into society as reformed and productive citizens. The Iligan City Jail serves not only as a place of incarceration but also as a potential stepping stone towards a positive future.
